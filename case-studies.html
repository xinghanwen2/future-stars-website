Baseline Hitting Analysis

Case Study: Player A, Age Unknown

Session Type: Baseline hitting evaluation

Views Collected: Side view and back view

Environments: Tee work and front toss

Current Status: Baseline report completed; retest comparison can be added later.

Big Picture Summary

Player A already has a workable swing foundation: complete swing motion, contact ability, repeatable general pattern, and strong training upside.

The main development priority is not whether Player A can hit the ball. The priority is improving how efficiently the body creates force.

What Player A Did Well

Complete Swing

Player A finishes the swing instead of stopping early.

Contact Ability

Player A can find the ball in tee work and moving-ball front toss.

Repeatability

The general swing shape repeats well enough for before/after comparison.

Effort and Intent

Player A swings with commitment rather than simply poking at the ball.

Main Improvement Areas

PriorityMeaning
Back-Hip LoadSit into the back hip more deeply before the swing starts.
Front-Side BlockAfter stride, the front side should brace more firmly so energy does not leak forward.
Hip-Led RotationThe hips should begin the turn before the upper body opens.
Head StabilityThe head should stay quieter through contact.

Simple Scorecard

CategoryScore

Recommended Drills

Pause Load Drill

Load into the back hip, pause for one second, then swing.

Stride and Hold Drill

Stride, land, hold for two seconds, then swing.

Hit and Freeze Drill

After contact, freeze for one second and keep the eyes near the contact zone.
