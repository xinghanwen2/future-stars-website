Project Overview

More than a website or one-time clinic

Future Stars Baseball Club is designed as a repeatable player development system. The goal is not simply to record swings or post videos. The goal is to help players and parents understand what is happening in the swing, identify clear improvement areas, apply simple drills, and track progress over time.

The project connects baseball coaching, structured video review, data organization, player reports, parent communication, and public anonymous case studies into one clear development process.

Project Mission

Help young baseball players ages 6–18 improve their hitting mechanics through clear visual feedback, simple development goals, and a structured before/after improvement process.

  • Make swing issues easier for players to understand
  • Give parents clear and practical feedback
  • Use video and data to support coaching decisions
  • Create a repeatable process for future players

Program Workflow

The Future Stars Development Loop

Each player should move through the same basic process: registration, consent, video capture, analysis, report, training plan, retest, and public case study only if approved.

1. Register

Parent registration collects basic player information, goals, and contact details.

2. Consent

Parent permission controls whether videos, photos, or case studies can be used.

3. Record

Players are recorded from standard angles during tee work and front toss.

4. Analyze

The swing is reviewed for balance, timing, bat path, contact point, and repeatability.

5. Report

A simple player report explains strengths, improvement areas, and next steps.

6. Train

The player receives 2–3 targeted drills based on the main swing issue.

7. Retest

A follow-up session compares baseline and retest clips using the same method.

8. Case Study

Approved cases may become anonymous public examples on the website.

Development Method

How Future Stars evaluates players

Future Stars uses a simple, repeatable analysis method so each player can be reviewed in a consistent way. The method focuses on the movement patterns that young players can understand and improve.

  • Two-angle video recording: side view and back/catcher view
  • Tee work and front toss evaluation
  • Review of balance, timing, bat path, contact point, and finish
  • Simple scorecards for key development areas
  • 2–3 drills connected to the player’s main issue
  • Retest plan to compare progress over time

Why this method matters

Many young players hear general advice like “stay balanced” or “keep your hands back,” but they do not always know what that means in their own swing. Video analysis helps make the issue visible, and a short drill plan turns the feedback into something the player can practice.

Privacy & Data Management

How player materials are handled

Because Future Stars works with youth players, privacy must be part of the project design. Public pages should show only approved, anonymous, and selected materials.

Material Type Recommended Location Public?
Raw videos Private Google Drive folders No
Full player reports Private Google Drive / shared with parent only No, unless redacted and approved
Selected clips or frames Website case study Only if approved
Anonymous case summaries Website Yes, if approved
Parent feedback Website or internal notes Only if approved
